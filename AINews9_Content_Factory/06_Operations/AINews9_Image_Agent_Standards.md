📱 AINews9 Mobile-First Visual Composition Standard (Version 2.0)
Use this section in every Featured Image Generation Brief.
________________________________________
Mobile-First Design Philosophy
This illustration is primarily designed for mobile readers, as most AINews9 visitors will consume content on smartphones.
The design must remain clear, readable, and visually impactful across:
•	Mobile phones
•	Tablets
•	Desktop browsers
•	WordPress Featured Images
•	Google Discover
•	Facebook previews
•	LinkedIn previews
•	WhatsApp sharing
•	X (Twitter) previews
The image should communicate its educational message within 2–3 seconds, even on a small mobile screen.
________________________________________
Safe Composition Zone
Place all critical visual elements inside the central 70% Safe Zone of the canvas.
Critical elements include:
•	Main Heading
•	Hindi Heading
•	Six AINews9 Personas
•	Central Illustration
•	Central Educational Message / Badge
•	Primary Workflow
•	Major Educational Icons
Avoid placing important content near the outer edges where social media platforms may crop the image.
No essential text or icons should appear within approximately 80 pixels of any canvas edge.
________________________________________
Visual Hierarchy
The illustration should follow this exact reading order:
1.	Main Heading
2.	Hindi Heading
3.	Central Illustration
4.	Six AINews9 Personas
5.	Central Educational Badge
6.	Educational Cards
7.	Workflow
8.	Bottom Educational Banner
9.	AINews9 Branding
The viewer should instantly understand the topic before noticing supporting details.
________________________________________
Eye Movement
Design the illustration so the viewer’s eyes naturally follow this path:
Main Heading
↓
Central Story
↓
Central Educational Badge
↓
Educational Cards
↓
Workflow
↓
Bottom Educational Banner
↓
AINews9 Branding
The composition should guide learning naturally without visual confusion.
________________________________________
Mobile Readability Rules
The illustration should remain understandable even when viewed at approximately 300–400 pixels wide.
At thumbnail size, the viewer should immediately recognize:
•	The article topic
•	The six recurring Indian personas
•	The central educational message
•	The primary safety or learning concept
Avoid depending on small text to communicate the main message.
________________________________________
Text Density Rules
Keep text intentionally minimal.
Maximum recommendations:
•	One main English heading
•	One Hindi heading
•	One English subheading
•	One Hindi subheading
•	Maximum 10–12 educational labels
•	Maximum 1–2 words per educational label
•	Maximum two lines inside any educational card
Avoid long explanatory sentences inside the illustration.
The article itself should provide the detailed explanation.
________________________________________
Educational Card Design
Educational cards should:
•	Be visually equal in size
•	Use large, easily recognizable icons
•	Maintain consistent spacing
•	Be readable independently
•	Never overlap with one another
•	Support the story rather than dominate it
Avoid tiny floating icons or cluttered information.
________________________________________
Negative Space
Maintain approximately 20–25% clean negative space throughout the illustration.
Allow generous breathing room between:
•	Personas
•	Workflow
•	Educational cards
•	Headings
•	Banner
•	Branding
A clean layout improves readability, especially on mobile devices.
________________________________________
Six Persona Guidelines
Always use the same recurring AINews9 personas:
👨‍🎓 Student
👩‍🏫 Teacher
👨‍💼 Professional
🛍️ Small Business Owner
👨‍👩‍👧 Parent
👵 Senior Citizen
The personas should:
•	Be immediately recognizable
•	Occupy a meaningful visual size
•	Participate naturally in the educational story
•	Display expressions appropriate to the article
•	Never appear as decorative background characters
They are the visual identity of AINews9.
________________________________________
Storytelling Principle
Every featured image should tell a single educational story.
Avoid showing people merely “using AI.”
Instead, illustrate a meaningful moment, such as:
•	Learning
•	Verifying
•	Comparing
•	Collaborating
•	Protecting privacy
•	Solving a problem
•	Teaching
•	Making a responsible decision
Each article should depict a unique human-centered scenario while maintaining a consistent visual identity.
________________________________________
Human-First AI Principle
AI should always appear as a supportive assistant, not the hero.
Humans should remain:
•	Thinking
•	Verifying
•	Learning
•	Deciding
•	Collaborating
Every illustration should reinforce the message:
People remain in control. AI provides assistance.
________________________________________
Thumbnail Recognition Test
Before considering the illustration complete, verify that it remains understandable at thumbnail size.
A reader should immediately recognize:
•	The article topic
•	The six personas
•	The educational action
•	The central message
If these are not immediately visible, simplify the composition.
________________________________________
Editorial Quality Standard
Every featured image should resemble a premium educational infographic suitable for publication in a leading technology or business magazine.
The visual style should be:
•	Bright
•	Trustworthy
•	Human-centric
•	Educational
•	Clean
•	Optimistic
•	Modern
•	Accessible
Avoid:
•	Clutter
•	Fear-based imagery
•	Overly futuristic visuals
•	Excessive visual effects
•	Decorative elements that distract from learning
________________________________________
Final Visual Goal
Every AINews9 featured image should answer four questions within the first few seconds:
1.	What is this article about?
2.	Who is it for?
3.	What action should the reader take?
4.	What is the key educational takeaway?
If all four answers are immediately clear, the illustration successfully supports AI literacy.
